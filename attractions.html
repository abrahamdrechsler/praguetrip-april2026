Valdštejn Castle
Gothic castle ruins on sandstone cliff · est. 1260
✅ Walkable Kid-Friendly ⚠️ Check April hours
One of the oldest castles in Český Ráj, perched on sandstone rocks with a Baroque chapel and rock-carved rooms. The walk from Turnov is the real attraction — a beautiful forest trail via the Hlavatice viewpoint with panoramic views of the rock formations.
Distance
4 km
Drive
8 min
Walk
~50 min
Without Car
Doable ✅
🥾
Walking Route
Follow the RED trail from Turnov square → Hlavatice viewpoint (1.4 km) → Valdštejn (2.9 km more). Gentle forest path. Kids can manage if you go slow — bring snacks.
👶
Kid Factor
Accessible, not too steep. The castle has nooks and crannies to explore. Rock formations are visually exciting for kids. Thomas (4) can do the walk with breaks.
📅
April Season
Typically opens weekends in April. The grounds and trail are accessible even if the castle interior is closed. Check hrad-valdstejn.cz closer to date.
🍺
Beer Nearby
Small refreshment stand near the castle in season. Better beer options back in Turnov.
Verdict: Must-do. The walk itself is the highlight — combine Valdštejn with the Hlavatice viewpoint as a half-day family outing. If kids are fresh, you can continue to Hrubá Skála (2.5 km more) or drive there separately.

Hrubá Skála Castle + Rock City
Renaissance château on sandstone cliffs · Iconic Český Ráj views
6.5 km · 12 min drive Kid-Friendly Trails Open Year-Round
The postcard image of Český Ráj. A château (now a hotel) perched on massive sandstone pillars, surrounded by a "rock city" of towering stone formations with walking trails weaving through narrow passages and viewpoints. The rock formations are the main attraction — the castle is secondary.
Distance
6.5 km
Drive
12 min
Walk from Turnov
~1.5 hrs
Without Car
Train + 25 min uphill walk
🚂
Without a Car
Train from Turnov to Hrubá Skála station (limited frequency), then 20–25 min uphill walk. Or walk from Turnov via Valdštejn (~6.5 km trail) — beautiful but long for kids. Pre-booked taxi ~€10–15 each way.
👶
Kid Factor
The rock trails have short and long loops. Short loop (~30 min) is very doable. Kids love the narrow passages between rocks, stairs carved into stone, and peekaboo viewpoints. Some sections have metal railings.
🏗️
Architecture Note
The château itself is a Renaissance rebuild on medieval foundations, dramatically perched on sandstone. Now an EA Hotel — you can visit the courtyard and tower for views. The geology-meets-architecture angle is right up Abe's alley.
🔗
Combine With
Valdštejn Castle (2.5 km trail between them). Drive to Hrubá Skála, walk the rock city, then hike to Valdštejn and back — or vice versa.
Verdict: Must-do, top priority. This is the defining experience of Český Ráj. The rock city trails are magical. Plan a full morning here. Combine with Valdštejn for a full day.

Šťastná země (Happy Land)
Radvánovice · Kids' adventure park with garnet mining
7 km · 10 min drive ✅✅ Best for Kids ⚠️ May NOT be open Apr 10–14
Massive outdoor kids' paradise: wooden castle with towers, air trampolines, a dwarf village, sand mine where kids dig for real Czech garnets, contact zoo, Celtic village, geopark trail, observation tower with water slide. Purpose-built for exactly your kids' ages.

Critical: The park typically opens late April or May. Your dates (Apr 10–14) may be too early. Check stastnazeme.cz or their Facebook page before going. This could be a total miss.

This is THE kid destination. Eloise (6) will love the wooden castle, climbing frames, and garnet mining. Thomas (4) will love the contact zoo, trampolines, and dwarf village. You could easily spend 3–4 hours here.

Verdict: Would be #1 priority for the kids — IF it's open. Check the season before planning around it. If closed, the garnet-mining experience is a loss with no real substitute.

Trosky Castle
Twin volcanic towers · The icon of Český Ráj
13 km · 18 min drive ⚠️ Steep climb ⚠️ Check April hours
The most recognizable silhouette in Bohemia — two volcanic basalt towers ("Baba" and "Panna") rising from the landscape, crowned with medieval castle ruins. Dramatic and photogenic. But the climb to the towers is steep and there's no way around it.

Mixed. Eloise (6) could probably do it with encouragement. Thomas (4) might struggle with the steep stone stairs to the towers. You might need to carry him for sections, which at 6'8" on narrow medieval stairs... consider carefully. The view from below is still impressive.
🚌
Without a Car
Bus to Rovensko pod Troskami + 5 km walk to the castle. Or train + bus with transfers (~1h 40m). Not practical with kids. Taxi ~€20–25 each way.

Typically open April weekends only. Check hrad-trosky.cz. Even if closed, you can see the towers from outside — worth the drive for the view alone.

Verdict: Worth seeing, but manage expectations. The drive alone is beautiful. If the castle is open, adults and Eloise can climb while Allie/grandparent stays with Thomas. Don't force it with a tired 4-year-old.

Prachovské skály (Prachov Rocks)
Sandstone rock city · Trails through stone labyrinths
20 km · 25 min drive Kid-Friendly Trails Open April
The largest and most famous "rock city" in Český Ráj — a labyrinth of sandstone towers, narrow passages, viewpoints, and forested trails. Two marked loops: a shorter one (~1 hr) and a longer one (~2 hrs). Different vibe than Hrubá Skála — more expansive, more "lost in a stone forest" feeling.
Distance
20 km
Drive

Kid Factor
Short loop is very doable with kids. Narrow passages between rocks are thrilling — kids squeeze through while adults duck. Some stairs but manageable. Thomas (4) will love hiding in rock crevices.
🆚
vs. Hrubá Skála
Hrubá Skála has the castle + dramatic perch. Prachov Rocks are more about immersion in the rock formations themselves — more trails, more variety, bigger area. If you can only do one, Hrubá Skála. If you have time, Prachov is worth a half-day.
💰
Admission
Small entrance fee (~80–120 CZK/adult). Parking available at the entrance.
Verdict: Great half-day trip if you have the car and a free morning. Not essential if you've already done Hrubá Skála, but a different and worthwhile experience. The short loop is perfect for kids.

Prague Castle (Pražský hrad)
Largest castle complex in the world · ~15 min walk uphill
Walk from apartment Manage kid energy Abe knows this
You're staying at the base of the castle hill. Walk up Nerudova street (past the in-laws' place!) to the castle. St. Vitus Cathedral, Golden Lane, the gardens. Abe's been twice — focus on what's new: the kids' experience, the southern gardens (often overlooked), the Royal Garden.
Walk
~15 min uphill
Time Needed
2–3 hrs
Verdict: Unavoidable and worth doing — you literally live at its doorstep. Go early morning before crowds. Golden Lane is touristy but kids enjoy the tiny houses. Don't try to do everything — pick 2-3 things.
Petřín Hill
Park, lookout tower, mirror maze, funicular · ~10 min walk to base
Walk from apartment ✅✅ Great for Kids Year-Round
A huge green hill rising right behind your apartment. Take the funicular up (kids love it), visit the mini Eiffel Tower (Petřín Lookout Tower — 299 steps!), the mirror maze (Zrcadlové bludiště — hilarious for kids), then walk down through orchards and gardens. Playground at the base.
Walk to base
~10 min
Time Needed
2–3 hrs
Verdict: Top-3 kid activity in Prague. Funicular + tower + mirror maze is a perfect combo. Go on a clear day for the views. Easy bail point — if kids are tired, just walk downhill through the park back to the apartment.
Kampa Island & Lennon Wall
Park, Lennon Wall, David Černý babies, watermill · 5 min walk
5 min walk Great for Kids Free
A peaceful island park right next to your apartment, separated from Malá Strana by a narrow channel ("Čertovka" — Devil's Stream). Kids can run around on the grass, see the giant crawling baby sculptures by David Černý, and you can stroll past the historic watermill. The Lennon Wall is right there too — bring chalk!
Walk
5 min
Time Needed
30 min–1 hr
Verdict: Not a destination per se — more of a daily hang. Perfect for jet-lag day, before/after dinner stroll, or whenever kids need to run. Kampa Museum has modern art if adults want culture while kids play in the park.
Charles Bridge (Karlův most)
Gothic stone bridge · 30 Baroque statues · ~5 min walk
5 min walk Abe knows this Crowded midday
You'll cross this every time you go to Old Town. The key is WHEN: go at sunrise (~6:15 AM in April) or after 8 PM for the empty, magical experience. Midday it's a tourist gauntlet. Touch the brass plaque of St. John of Nepomuk for good luck — kids can reach it. Climb the Old Town Bridge Tower for incredible views.
Walk
5 min
Best Time
Sunrise or after 8 PM
Verdict: You'll see it regardless — it's between you and Old Town. One early morning trip (Abe solo? or with FIL for coffee?) to see it empty is worth it. Bridge Tower is worth the climb.
Short Transit / Tram Ride
Vyšehrad
Ancient hilltop fortress · Quieter alternative to Prague Castle
~20 min by tram Kid-Friendly Great Playground
The "other castle" that most tourists skip. Ancient fortress on a cliff above the Vltava with incredible river views, a Romanesque rotunda, the famous cemetery (Dvořák, Mucha, Smetana), casemates (underground tunnels), and — crucially — a really good playground inside the fortress walls. Much less crowded than Prague Castle.
Getting There
Tram + short walk
Time Needed
2–3 hrs
Verdict: Highly recommended for Abe specifically — less touristy, deeper history, incredible architecture. The playground makes it a family win. FIL will like the rampart walks and views. Go on a day when you want to escape the Malá Strana/Old Town bubble.
Střelecký ostrov (Shooters Island)
River island park with playground · 10 min walk
10 min walk Great Playground Free
Small island in the Vltava with a well-maintained playground, café, and views of the National Theatre and the bridges. Accessed from Most Legií bridge. Perfect mid-day break point — kids play while adults sit with coffee/beer. Much less known than Kampa.
Walk
10 min
Time Needed
1–2 hrs
Verdict: Perfect playground break. When kids are melting down during a day of sightseeing, detour here. Beer for adults, swings for kids, everyone recharges.
National Technical Museum
Trains, planes, cars, machines · Indoor rainy-day option
~15 min tram Great for Kids Rainy Day
Enormous hall filled with vintage cars, motorcycles, steam trains, biplanes, and a printing press. Thomas (4) will lose his mind. Interactive exhibits, a simulated mine in the basement. One of the best rainy-day options in Prague for families.
Getting There
Tram 22 → Letná
Time Needed
2–3 hrs
Verdict: Keep in your back pocket for a rainy day. Thomas-specific win. Combine with a walk through Letná Park (beer garden with views!) afterward if weather clears.
Letná Park & Beer Garden
Best views in Prague + best beer garden · ~20 min walk or tram
Beer + Views Playground Abe + FIL
Expansive hilltop park above the Vltava with THE panoramic view of Prague's bridges. The beer garden (Letná Beer Garden) has cheap draft beer, sausages, and an unbeatable sunset view. There's a playground in the park. The Prague Metronome (giant ticking metronome where Stalin's statue once stood) is an architecture conversation starter.
Getting There
20 min walk or tram
Time Needed
1–2 hrs
Verdict: Abe + FIL beer pilgrimage. Late afternoon — grab a bench, watch the sunset over the bridges with a Pilsner. Kids play in the park. Everyone wins. Combine with the Technical Museum if weather cooperates.
