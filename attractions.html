Hrubá Skála Castle + Rock City
Renaissance château on sandstone cliffs · Iconic Český Ráj views
4 mi · 12 min drive Kid-Friendly Trails Open Year-Round
The postcard image of Český Ráj. A château (now a hotel) perched on massive sandstone pillars, surrounded by a "rock city" of towering stone formations with walking trails weaving through narrow passages and viewpoints. The rock formations are the main attraction — the castle is secondary.

Malá Skála
Jizera river village · Rock formations, cliffside chapel, riverside walks
✅ Train 9 min Walkable along river Open Daily
A scenic village on the Jizera river, just 9 minutes by train from Turnov (or a 2.5 mi walk along the riverbank). The area packs in three great things: the Klokočské skály sandstone rock formations to the southwest (trails through towers and passages, similar to Hrubá Skála but wilder and quieter), a small white chapel perched dramatically on a cliff above the village with panoramic views over the Jizera valley, and the river itself for a peaceful walk. Note: Frýdštejn Castle is 2 km further up the valley but doesn't open until May 1 — you can hike to it and view it from outside.

Klokočské Skály
Sandstone rock formations to the southwest of the village. Trails wind through towers and narrow passages with viewpoints over the Jizera valley. Similar experience to Hrubá Skála's rock city — quieter, wilder, and free to explore. Easy to spend 2 hours wandering.
⛪

A small white chapel perched on a sandstone cliff above the village — one of the most photogenic spots in the region. Short steep climb to reach it. Great views over the Jizera valley. Worth the scramble.

Train ride is exciting for kids. Rock trails are adventurous and mostly flat with some scrambling. Chapel climb is steep but short. River walk back is easy. Thomas (4) should manage fine with breaks.
Verdict: Best easy day trip from Turnov — no car, 9 min train, great rock hiking, photogenic chapel, peaceful river walk back. Do this on a weekday when Valdštejn/Hrubá Skála are less practical.

Rotštejn Castle (Hrad Rotštejn)
Gothic ruin on a sandstone rock · Forest walk from Turnov or Malá Skála
✅ Walkable from Turnov ⚠️ Weekends only in April ⚠️ Closes in rain
A small Gothic castle ruin perched on a sandstone outcrop deep in the Klokočské skály forest, reachable by a beautiful forest trail from Turnov or combinable with a Malá Skála day (train in, walk to castle via the rocks). The appeal is the journey as much as the destination — forest paths, sandstone formations, and a quiet, non-touristy ruin at the end. Only open weekends in April, and closes immediately in bad weather (plus 4 hours after rain stops for safety).

~1.5–2 hrs one way

🥾
Getting There
From Turnov: follow forest trails through Klokočské skály (~1.5–2 hrs). Or take the train to Malá Skála (9 min), then hike through the rocks to Rotštejn (~45 min). The Malá Skála combo is a full-day loop: train in → Klokočské skály rocks → Rotštejn → chapel → walk back along the river.
👶
Kid Factor
The forest walk is doable but long for Thomas (4) — consider the train-in option which shortens the hike. The castle itself is a small ruin with a tower to climb. Kids enjoy the "hidden in the woods" feeling. Bring snacks.
☁️
Weather Warning
The castle closes for ANY rain — even showers — and stays closed for 4 hours after the rain stops (falling rocks risk). Check the weather forecast the night before and call ahead if in doubt. April in Bohemia can be unpredictable.
Verdict: A lovely, uncrowded alternative to Valdštejn — same "Gothic ruin in the forest" vibe but quieter and wilder. Only works on Apr 11 or 12 (your only weekend days in Turnov) and only in dry weather. Call 721 195 471 morning-of to confirm it's open.

Granát Turnov — Czech Garnet Cooperative
Palackého 240, Turnov · Garnet workshop + showroom · ~5 min walk
✅ 5 min walk Kid-Friendly Year-Round
Turnov has been the center of Czech garnet jewelry since 1953. The Granát cooperative's HQ is right in town — you can visit their showroom and watch traditional garnet-cutting and setting. Czech pyrope garnet (Bohemian garnet) is the dark red stone in traditional Czech jewelry, and Turnov is where most of it is crafted. If Allie or Denise wants to buy authentic garnet jewelry direct from the source, this is the place — not a tourist shop.

Classical Concert in a Church
~1 hour · Organ, strings, or chamber ensemble · Multiple venues
Parents / Adults Under $30/person ~1 hr, no intermission
Prague's historic churches host short classical concerts almost daily — Bach, Vivaldi, Mozart, Dvořák. Best options:

⭐ Klementinum Mirror Chapel (Zrcadlová kaple) — the top recommendation. A tiny Baroque jewel in Old Town lined floor-to-ceiling with gilded mirrors and frescoes. Intimate chamber concerts (strings + harpsichord) in one of the most beautiful small rooms in Europe. Book at klementinum.com. ~490–690 CZK. Old Town, 20 min walk from apartment.

St. Nicholas, Malá Strana — literally outside your front door. Massive Baroque interior, thundering historic organ. Book at stnicholas.cz. ~500–700 CZK.
St. Martin in the Wall — consistently best-reviewed. Off the tourist drag, intimate, great acoustics. ~400–600 CZK.

⚠️ Avoid buying from costumed ticket sellers on the street — tourist trap. Book online or at the venue box office.
